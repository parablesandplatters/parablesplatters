Let's go

Pap

  • 1L water
  • 2 cups maize meal
  • 2 cups water for mixing
  • Salt (to taste)
  • Whisk for stirring

1. Boil the 1L water in a pot.

2. In a separate bowl, mix maize meal with 2 cups of water.

3. Gradually stir the maize meal mixture into the boiling water.

4. Stir continuously to avoid lumps. Be patient!

5. Let it cook on medium heat for 10 minutes.

6. Adjust thickness by adding more maize meal as needed. Cook for an additional 5 minutes for perfect consistency.

Enjoy your delicious pap!

Pasta

  • Lots of water
  • Seasoning
  • Olive oil to prevent sticking
  • Salt, pepper, and olive oil for seasoning

1. Boil plenty of water in a pot and add seasoning.

2. Add olive oil to prevent the pasta from sticking.

3. Once the water boils, add the pasta and stir gently with tongs.

4. Let it cook until 'al dente' (test by biting a strand).

5. Drain the pasta and mix with olive oil, salt, and pepper for a perfect dish!

Serve with steak and wors for a complete meal!

Braai Wors

  • start your fire by putting the charcoal on top of the grill and then putting paper, cardboard and plastic or just paper alone - anything you find, and then put these underneath with your grill of charcoal on top, and then light it up. the charcoal will burn perfectly from underneath.
  • once you see your charcoal being red, load it inside the stand carefully.
  • clean your grill with half an onion.
  • let it heat up some more and then once you got red hot flaming charcoal all over, you would have already marinaded your wors with spices of your choosing, and then place it on your grill to cook.
  • With wors, we always want to limit flames to avoid the skin burning black, so always have your cup of water on standby to sprinkle.
  • as your wors is braaing, do some small cuts to it to allow to cook thoroughly on the inside. keep turning it, and then once you're satisfied, you're all done with Wors.
