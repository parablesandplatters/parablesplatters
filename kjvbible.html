About the KJV Holy Bible

The King James Version (KJV) is a revered English translation of the Bible, commissioned in 1604 and published in 1611.

  • Historical Origin: Commissioned by King James I to resolve religious differences and unify worship across England.
  • Translation Team: Produced by 47 scholars using early Greek and Hebrew manuscripts.
  • Legacy: It has profoundly influenced literature, theology, and the English language.

The KJV's poetic style and phrasing have shaped English expressions and inspired generations of Christians worldwide. Its accuracy, reverence, and timeless beauty continue to resonate today.
